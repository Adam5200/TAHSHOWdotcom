The Soundtrack

The Soundtrack is the most important element within the entire show. It itself was the inspiration to create The Adam Hendrickson Show, and exists within the story to some degree. Naturally, there must be a page on this website dedicated to this music. Here, I've included 13 tracks from the show, and they're good ones too! If it were up to me I would post every single one but that's well over 100 tracks. Maybe someday! There are currently 3 TAHS Soundtrack album volumes out on Apple Music, Spotify and other streaming services.

The Main Titles

This was the first piece of music I ever wrote for the show, all the way back in 2014. The main melody at least, and the main idea of the whole thing. I love those bouncing piano chords, they almost shuffle in a way. I always knew that the honky tonk piano would be a very important instrument within the show, and this is the first time we hear it. The main theme exists like this for the first 5 episodes, at which point Adam begins creating The Whitelake Soundtrack, and the theme is different in every episode from that point.

Episode 1 Track 2 - "Come In"

After I had conceptualized the idea of The Storyteller (though I did not know his name yet), i still knew it had to sing a song in the beginning of the show. I struggled with the idea for a long time, but eventually I thought the name of the song should be "Come In". So I turned that into a melody (the first four notes of the song). Everything else came pretty much naturally after that. Another thing I knew is that this song would feature an accordion, which you can hear in the third verse "and now you're here at whitelake...". Of course, this song features Elliot Jean, the voice actor for The Storyteller. This was the first thing he ever recorded for the show, and as soon as I heard it, I knew I had found the perfect voice.

Episode 1 Track 12 - Whitelake Tour

The theme song is definitely the first thing I created for real, like musically. But this is the first track I ever conceptualized. I knew that in the first episode of The Adam Hendrickson Show, there would be a tour given at the end of Episode 1. I also knew the tour guide would end up being the soundbooth technician, who eventually introduces Adam to the chamber choir. The structure of the tour hasn't changed much since I first envisioned it. This particular "version" of "The Tour Track" was written in 2019. Prior to this, the "in-place" track was more or less the main theme with variation, similiar in structure. That piece of music was later re-made and used (not on this web page) as "Adam's Life Story" in Episode 2.

Episode 3 Track 4 - Keyboarding Class

This track starts out actually with a softened reprise of a track heard, when Adam and Arjun meet up. We hear it again here, perhaps because Arjun and Adam are in this particular scene. I think though, this time we hear it, it has a proper ending, as it gets cut off in episode 1. At this point, visually, Adam goes into his keyboarding class. For this, I recorded a sort of "shy" version of the whitelake theme, and improvised on it until I could bring it to "Come In", for when The Storyteller appears. I love the echo on the synths during that quiet bit in the middle. I've had one viewer tell me that this is their favorite track.

Episode 4 Track 3 - Adam's Dream

This track takes place during the first dream sequence, in Episode 4. Here, Adam runs into the mysterious character Foster, who he has seen hanging around the girl of his interest, Victoria. Foster begins telling Adam something, telling him to change his path, but the dialogue is stilted, and dreamlike, Adam can't really make it out. The Storyteller appears, and essentially tells Foster to back off. Foster responds by explaining he has never seen The Storyteller before. Foster attempts to make Adam aware of The Storyteller, but as Adam turns around to look, he wakes up. A fun fact about this track. After the intro section, the next part that is faster, the chords I used for that sequence, are not the chords from Come In, but the keys, like from the key changes in Come In. F minor, F# minor, and G minor, in that order.

Episode 4 Track 5 - Meeting Mr. Kristoff

This is another theme I wrote when I was actually in high school, and it always had the same function. To represent my wacky choir teacher. There really isn't much to say, since it is such a simple melody. Before The Show, I had a version of this track that was very similiar, though a little shorter, and much less professional sounding. I knew WHERE the track was gonna be in the show, so when I wrote the dialogue for that scene, I figured what I had written already would "line up". I was pretty much right, I only had to add in a measure or two to capture the scene correctly.

Episode 4 Track 10 - Your Faithful Storyteller

This is one of the slowest tracks, and definitely one of the most boring I've written for the show. Why would I put it here if it's boring you might wonder? Well, because I still think it's great. I love how basic it is. It gives you a chance to single out particular instruments, and hear them in near full quality. At the end of the day, this track doesn't exist for some superb reason. It was made to be music behind The Storyteller giving his little speech near the end of this episode. But it is still appreciated nonetheless!

Episode 5 Track 5 - The Nice Cream House

There is a particular chord-based theme that I've always thought to be "the romance theme". You can hear it getting used in all sorts of ways in this track, where Adam and Victoria are out on their first date at The Nice Cream House. This moment in the show marks a new point in Adam's life, as his heart begins to activate essentially, and it is reflected here in the music. It sounds almost disconnected from the rest of the show's music, but eventually, it always finds it's way back there. Perhaps the love he feels for Victoria begins to affect his composition skills, for the better. The end of this track also includes the score for part of the scene afterwards, when Adam is reciting these events to Amber in the soundbooth. The music gets a bit more "Come In"-y as we get closer to the soundbooth. However, their conversation soon turns to the subject of Foster, and his mysterious journal...

Episode 5 Track 7 - Cryptic Foster

Following the previous track listed, Adam and Amber discuss Foster, before Amber gets called to the stage, leaving Adam alone in the booth. When this track begins, that is when Foster enters. This isn't the first time the two have met. Foster begins asking questions, and insinuiating that he is aware of some great knowledge, and that it is contained also in his journal.

Episode 6 Track 5 - The Perfect Gift

This, again is more of an incidental piece. It begins with the chords of the main theme, in a weird key, so you don't hear it at first. The track soon transforms to a "Come In" reprise. This scene is when Adam is trying to figure out what to get Victoria for their one month anniversary. The Storyteller appears, and as he speaks to us, Adam gets new ideas. As we switch between Adam and The Storyteller, the motif changes. For The Storyteller, it is always "Come In", but for Adam, there is a mix of the main theme, and the whitelake theme. At the end of the track, we hear the main theme again, much more regally, with the original key and piano sound, marching along. Adam found the perfect gift idea.

Episode 7 Track 5 - The Abandoned Pool

For this unique part of the show, THIS track I feel best represents the style of music I decided on for this episode. There is lots of atmospheric elements, but we can hear the all-familiar TAHS sounds sprinkled in here and there, just to remind you that even though this episode is totally strange and seemingly unrelated.. it is still truly The Adam Hendrickson Show. The ending of this track is one of my favorite part of this whole soundtrack, with that bouncing piano. That theme originates way back in like 2015, when I wanted to come up with a theme for "The Gang".

Episode 8 Track 8 - The Storyteller Modifies

This is where the show really starts to take shape. Once The Storyteller does what he does in this scene, we know that things will never ever be the same for Adam or Victoria. The theme we start out with in this track is actually going to be properly introduced in a future episode, so I won't talk about it here. But that finds it's way to the main theme... which finds it's way to "Come In", and THAT's where the song kicks off. After that, there are no rules. There is no description other than the audio file itself.

Episode 9 Track 7 - "Marvelous Musical World"

For this song, you have to keep in mind what this show means. You have to keep in mind the implication that "The Adam Hendrickson Show" is really just the end result of the events of the show. Something about that moment, when Adam is standing up on stage with the rest of the choir for the first time, looking out into the Whitelake auditorium. I'd say something about being there, and looking out into those seats and remembering sitting down in them on day one, with no idea that any of this would happen to him. And he feels so happy about it because it gives him all this energy to create music, and so he does.
